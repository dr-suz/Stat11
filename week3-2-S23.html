2. Modeling Two Numeric Variables with the Line of Best Fit

Sum of squares notation

\[s_x = \sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_i - \bar{x})^2 \quad s_x = \sum_{i=1}^{n}(y_i - \bar{y})^2, \quad s_{xy} = \sum_{i=1}^{n}(x_i - \bar{x})(y_i-\bar{y})\]

least squares model coefficients

\[b_1 = r \frac{s_y}{s_x}\quad\text{and}\quad b_0 = \bar{y} - b_1 \bar{x}\] slope will change from one sample to the next; changes in one unit of \(x\) associated with changes of \(b_1\) units in \(y\)

\[\hat{y} = b_0 + b_1 x\]

Data consisting of foot length and height for 20 individuals

Assessing the residuals

\[\text{Residual} = e = y - \hat{y} = \text{observed value} - \text{predicted value}\]

standard deviation of the residuals \[s_e = \frac{\sum e^2 }{n-1}\]

Normal model (and 68-95-99.7 rule) applies when assumptions are reasonable

3. Summary Statistics for Simple Linear Regression

Slope

Correlation coefficient

is the number of std devs by which one variable is expected to change for a one standard deviation change in the other

is unitless

will change from one sample to the next

For a sample of size \(n\), with a numeric variable \((x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n)\), and another numeric variable, \((y_1, y_2, \dots, y_n)\), we can calculate the sample correlation by finding: \[r = \frac{s_{xy}}{\sqrt{s_x s_y}}.\]

Coefficient of determination

is the square of the correlation coefficient

is the fraction/proportion of the variation/variability in \(y\) accounted for by a linear model of \(x\)

reflects the successfullness of the model at relating \(y\) to \(x\) (goodness of fit measure)

For simple linear regression: \(R^2 = r^2\)
